Our Scientific Approach

6-Framework Conflict Resolution Model

We combine proven psychological frameworks to identify the real sources of disagreement — not just the surface-level arguments — and build a personalised resolution pathway.

1

Interest-Based Relational (IBR) Model

Developed at Harvard. Separates positions ("I want engineering") from interests ("I want job security"). Most conflicts exist because families argue positions, not interests. We uncover the real motivations.

2

Holland RIASEC Career Model

Dr. John Holland's scientifically validated framework maps personality types (Realistic, Investigative, Artistic, Social, Enterprising, Conventional) to career families — objectively matching aptitude to courses.

3

Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Instrument

Identifies whether each person is Competing, Accommodating, Avoiding, Compromising, or Collaborating — revealing the power dynamics behind the disagreement and the healthiest resolution style.

4

Cognitive Behavioral Analysis

Identifies cognitive distortions driving the conflict: catastrophizing ("you'll fail"), all-or-nothing thinking ("only IIT matters"), social comparison ("neighbour's son did CS"). We surface hidden beliefs.

5

SMART Goal Alignment

Maps both parties' 3, 5, and 10-year visions using Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound criteria — revealing that most families actually share the same long-term goals but disagree on the path.

6

Maslow's Hierarchy Applied to Education

Parents often prioritize Security (stable job, ROI). Students prioritize Self-Actualization (passion, creativity). Neither is wrong — they're at different hierarchy levels. We bridge the gap.

Takes 12–18 minutes · Both parent & student should participate
