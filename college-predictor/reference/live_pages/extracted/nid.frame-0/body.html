Step-by-Step NID Admission Process

Two-stage DAT process: Prelims → Mains → Counselling → Admission

1

Online Registration

Visit admissions.nid.edu and create an account. Fill in personal, educational, and contact details. Upload photograph, signature, and category certificates.

2

Pay Application Fee

General/OBC/EWS: ₹3,000 | SC/ST/PwD: ₹1,500 | Overseas: ₹5,000. Pay via UPI, net banking, or card.

3

DAT Prelims Exam

3-hour written test with MCQ + subjective questions. Tests design aptitude, creativity, visualization, observation skills, and general awareness.

4

Prelims Result

B.Des results: April 7, 2026. Only 1.5× candidates (of total seats) get shortlisted for Mains. Prelims score is NOT counted in final merit.

5

DAT Mains - Studio Test

For B.Des: Hands-on studio test — sketching, model-making, design exercises. For M.Des: Studio test + personal interview. Tests creativity under real constraints.

6

Final Result & Merit

Final merit based entirely on Mains performance. Category-wise merit lists published. Campus and course preferences considered.

7

Counselling & Seat Allotment

Expected June 2026. Submit campus preferences, verify documents, pay token fee. Round 2 for vacant seats follows.

8

Report to Campus

Report with original documents to allotted NID campus. Pay semester fees. Begin your design journey!

Engineering vs NID — Which Path Is Right?

A detailed comparison to help students and parents make informed decisions

⚙ Engineering (B.Tech/B.E.)

  • 4-year technical degree via JEE/state CETs
  • Focus: Mathematics, Physics, Computer Science
  • Structured curriculum with labs & practicals
  • Career: Software, Core Engineering, Research
  • Average package (top IITs): ₹15-25 LPA
  • Thinking style: Analytical & logical
  • Job market: Very competitive, large candidate pool
  • Higher studies: M.Tech, MBA, MS abroad
  • Work style: Code, calculate, optimize systems
  • Entrance: JEE Main/Advanced — highly math-intensive

🎨 NID Design (B.Des)

  • 4-year design degree via NID DAT
  • Focus: Creativity, visual thinking, design aptitude
  • Project-based learning with studio practice
  • Career: UX/UI, Product Design, Animation, Branding
  • Average package (NID Ahmedabad): ₹17-30 LPA
  • Thinking style: Creative & visual
  • Job market: Niche, high-demand, less competition
  • Higher studies: M.Des, international design schools
  • Work style: Sketch, prototype, solve human problems
  • Entrance: NID DAT — creativity & aptitude focused

NID DAT Sample Questions

Understand the type of questions asked in the NID Design Aptitude Test

DAT Prelims — Design Aptitude & Reasoning ▼

Q: Look at the sequence of shapes below. Which shape comes next in the pattern?
(Visual reasoning — pattern recognition with geometric shapes)

Q: Identify the odd one out from the following set of design objects and explain your reasoning.
(Tests visual classification and design awareness)

Q: What design principle is violated in the following product image? Suggest two improvements.
(Tests critical observation and design thinking)

DAT Prelims — General Awareness & Visualization ▼

Q: Who designed the logo of the Indian Railways? What design principle does it follow?
(Design history and general knowledge)

Q: A flat 2D shape is shown. Draw how it would appear as a 3D object when folded.
(Spatial visualization — very common in NID DAT)

Q: Describe the concept of "sustainable design" with two real-world examples.
(Current affairs + design awareness)

DAT Mains — Studio Test (B.Des) ▼

Q: Using the materials provided (paper, scissors, tape, colors), create a 3D model of a "public seating solution for elderly people in a park."
(Tests creative problem-solving with constraints)

Q: Draw a series of 6 frames illustrating "slow fashion and conscious buying" — tell a visual story without using any text.
(Visual storytelling and concept development)

Q: Redesign the packaging of a common Indian snack brand to appeal to health-conscious millennials.
(Applied design thinking with market awareness)

M.Des Interview — Typical Questions ▼

Q: Walk us through your design portfolio. What was your most challenging project and why?

Q: How would you approach redesigning the voting experience in rural India?

Q: What is the difference between "design thinking" and "art"? Give examples.

Tip: Interviewers prioritize clarity of thought, honesty, and the ability to articulate your design process over polished answers.

Overseas & NRI Students — Admission Guide

Complete procedure for international, NRI, OCI, and PIO candidates

Key Eligibility Rules

Overseas Category

15% supernumerary seats reserved in B.Des and M.Des programs. Foreign passport holders with valid PIO or OCI cards can apply under this category. Separate fee structure applies.

NRI with Indian Passport

NRIs holding an Indian passport are NOT eligible for the Overseas category. They must apply as Indian nationals and compete in the general merit list with Indian fee structure.

OCI / PIO Card Holders

OCI and PIO cardholders must apply under the Overseas (supernumerary) category only. They pay fees equivalent to foreign citizens. No age relaxation or scribe facility available.

Student Visa Requirement

Overseas candidates must obtain a valid student visa for the entire program duration before joining NID. This is mandatory before experiencing campus life.
