COMMON QUESTIONS
Frequently Asked
Can I do ACCA and CFA together?

Technically yes, but extremely demanding. Most candidates recommend completing one first. ACCA + CFA is a powerful combination for investment banking or CFO-level roles.

Is CFA harder than CA?

Both are rigorous in different ways. CA (India) has a very low pass rate (~10–20%) and mandatory articleship. CFA demands ~900 hours of self-study per level. CA is harder to pass; CFA demands more total study time.

Does ACCA give exemptions to graduates?

Yes. ACCA offers exemptions based on your degree. Accounting and finance graduates can receive up to 9 paper exemptions, significantly shortening the overall qualification period.

Which qualification pays the most?

CFA charterholders in senior investment roles often command the highest global salaries. ACCA and CA salaries vary by region. All three lead to strong earning potential at senior levels.

Can I study while working full-time?

ACCA is the most flexible — attempt papers one at a time. CFA requires intense dedicated study windows. CA's articleship is itself a full-time commitment for 3 years.

Which is best for Big 4 firms?

All three are respected at Big 4 (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG). ACCA and CA dominate in audit and tax. CFA is preferred for Big 4 advisory or transactions divisions.
