CHAPTER ONE
Introduction to Engineering Admissions for NRIs
CHAPTER 1
Why Study Engineering in India?

For NRI families in the Gulf, India's top engineering colleges offer academic credibility, global placement value, and affordable education.

India's National Institutes of Technology (NITs), Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs), and Schools of Planning & Architecture (SPAs) are among Asia's most respected technical institutions. NIT graduates are recruited by leading global firms — and an NIT degree is widely recognised in the Gulf, UK, US, and Australia.

The Challenge Facing NRI Families
Q?
Quota Confusion
Many families are unaware of dedicated NRI quotas — DASA and CIWG — which sit outside the highly competitive domestic JEE pool.
$$
Fee Misinformation
Widespread misconception that NIT fees are prohibitively expensive, when CIWG students pay Indian-parity tuition — a major financial advantage.
Doc
Document Complexity
Multiple document types, strict deadlines, and verification requirements catch families off guard when managing applications from abroad.
Cal
Timeline Pressure
DASA runs on a tight calendar. Missing the JEE Main registration or DEF payment window means waiting an entire year.
Where DASA & CIWG Fit In

The Direct Admission of Students Abroad (DASA) scheme is run by the Ministry of Education (Government of India) and coordinated by NIT Rourkela. It creates a dedicated 15% supernumerary quota across NITs, IIITs, and CFTIs. Within this, a carved-out 5% is reserved for Children of Indian Workers in Gulf Countries (CIWG).

OK
KEY TAKEAWAY
DASA and CIWG are government-mandated, officially recognised pathways designed to make India's top engineering colleges accessible to students and families living abroad.

CHAPTER TWO
What is DASA & CIWG?
CHAPTER 2
DASA vs CIWG — Clearly Explained

Two categories, one portal, very different financial implications. This is the most important distinction for any NRI or Gulf-based family.

DASA — Direct Admission of Students Abroad

DASA is a scheme of the Ministry of Education, Government of India, administered through dasanit.org. It provides Foreign Nationals, OCI/PIO holders, and NRIs a dedicated pathway into UG engineering programs. Admission is based entirely on JEE (Main) rank. Tuition for DASA (Non-SAARC) candidates is charged in US Dollars.

CIWG — Children of Indian Workers in Gulf Countries

CIWG is a sub-category within DASA — not a separate scheme. It reserves 5% of seats for NRI students whose parent(s) work in one of 8 specified Gulf countries:

United Arab Emirates

KWT
Kuwait

The defining advantage of CIWG: tuition is paid at Indian Rupee rates — the same as domestic students — not in US Dollars. This creates substantial savings over four years.

IMPORTANT
OCI/PIO card holders are not eligible for CIWG seats, even if a parent works in the Gulf. CIWG is exclusively for NRIs. Always confirm your nationality status before choosing a category.

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
1. Studying Class 11-12 partly in India — must prove 2 full years abroad.

2. Missing the subject combination — IB/A-Level students often miss the third qualifying science subject.

3. Confusing OCI with CIWG — OCI holders are NOT eligible for CIWG regardless of Gulf employment.

4. Parent visa expired before 2025 — must be valid on at least one date during 2025.

CHAPTER FOUR
Step-by-Step Admission Process
CHAPTER 4
From JEE to Your College Seat

DASA has five clearly defined stages. Missing any step — even by a day — can end your application for that year.

01
Appear in JEE (Main) 2025
Register and appear in JEE (Main) 2025 — from India or international centres as announced by NTA. A valid JEE rank is mandatory for everything that follows.
02
Register on DASA Portal & Pay DEF
Visit dasanit.org. Pay the DASA Enrolment Fee (DEF) in one transaction. CIWG: US$300 + ₹62,500. DASA Non-SAARC: US$4,300 total. The US$300 registration fee is non-refundable.
03
Upload Documents & Verification
Upload all required documents. If a query is raised by the verification officer, respond within the stipulated portal deadline. Non-response = automatic cancellation.
04
Online Choice Filling & Seat Allotment (3 Rounds)
Fill institute-branch preferences in order. DASA runs 3 rounds. Allotment is based on JEE rank within your declared preference list.
05
Online Reporting & Physical Reporting at Institute
Complete online reporting on the portal to confirm your seat. Then physically report to the allotted institute with original documents and pay balance fees directly to the institute.
!
CRITICAL RULE
The DASA process is fully computerised and non-reversible. Missing the DEF payment window means you are out of the 2025 process with no exceptions or appeals.

CHAPTER SIX
Cut-offs & Seat Trends
CHAPTER 6
Understanding Cut-offs

DASA cut-offs are not announced in advance. They emerge from actual demand each round. Understanding how they work is more valuable than chasing exact numbers.

How DASA Cut-offs Work

A cut-off is the JEE (Main) rank of the last candidate admitted to a particular institute-branch-category in a given round. It changes every year based on four key factors:

NIT
Institute Reputation
Top NITs attract stronger candidates and have more competitive closing ranks.
CSE
Branch Demand
CSE and ECE consistently close at stronger ranks than Civil or Chemical at the same institute.
R3
Round Number
Later rounds may open seats unfilled in Round 1, sometimes at more accessible ranks.
Cat
Category
CIWG and DASA-General seats are separate pools with separate cut-offs. Check the correct category.
!
DISCLAIMER — CUT-OFFS CHANGE EVERY YEAR
Historic data helps build a probability range — not predictions. Always build a safe-moderate-reach shortlist. Never rely on a single year's figure.
Interpreting Historical Trends Wisely
V
Use 3-5 years of data to identify a trend band, not a single year
V
Compare both opening rank and closing rank
V
Track CIWG seats and DASA-General seats separately — they are different pools
!
Avoid any source claiming to "predict" exact cut-offs — no model is perfectly accurate

CHAPTER SEVEN
Fee Structure & CIWG Financial Advantage
CHAPTER 7
Official Fees — Accurate & Sourced

Fee information is one of the most misrepresented aspects of DASA. This chapter uses only officially confirmed data from the DASA 2025 Information Brochure.

DASA Enrolment Fee (DEF) — Official 2025 Figures

The DEF is paid centrally via the DASA portal. It is uniform across ALL NITs, IIITs, and IIEST — it does not vary by institute at this stage.

Candidate Tag	Registration Fee	Tuition Fee (Sem 1)	Total DEF
DASA-CIWG (Single Tag)	US$ 300	₹62,500	US$300 + ₹62,500
DASA Non-SAARC (NRI/OCI/PIO)	US$ 300	US$ 4,000	US$ 4,300
DASA SAARC (50% waiver)	US$ 300	US$ 2,000	US$ 2,300
Double Tag (CIWG + Non-SAARC)	US$ 300	US$ 4,000	US$ 4,300 (refund if CIWG allocated)
!
KEY CLARIFICATION
The first-semester DEF tuition is identical across all NITs — US$4,000 for DASA Non-SAARC and ₹62,500 for CIWG. There is no Top NIT vs Mid NIT difference in the centrally collected DASA fee. Fee differences between institutes appear only in Semesters 2-8, paid directly to each institute.
