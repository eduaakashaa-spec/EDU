The Root Cause · Psychology & Herd Mentality

Why Are NRI Students So Desperate for CSE?

Seven real reasons families make this choice — and the honest counter-argument for each one. Understanding the bias is the first step to making a better decision.

Myth 01 · "CSE = Guaranteed Job"

CSE guarantees a software job at any NIT

Parents believe CSE automatically means Google, Microsoft, or at least a decent IT role. The reality: CSE at NIT Jamshedpur places 45–55% students, many into Rs.6–9 LPA TCS/Infosys-type roles. The branch is CSE; the college still determines outcomes.

Reality Check
NIT Trichy ECE places 97.6% students. NIT Jamshedpur CSE places ~50%. Brand + branch both matter.
Myth 02 · "ECE/Mech Has No Future"

Core branches are dying; only CSE has a future

The semiconductor boom (India PLI), VLSI demand, embedded systems for EVs and IoT, and the Gulf petrochemical sector all require ECE/Mechanical/Chemical engineers. Global chip shortages 2020–24 created a 5-year talent gap that is still being filled.

Reality Check
Qualcomm, Broadcom, Nvidia recruit at NITT ECE on Day 1. Chemical Engg opens ADNOC/Aramco paths for Gulf families.
Myth 03 · "All NITs Are Equal"

A CSE degree from any NIT is the same

The NIT tag masks a 70-rank gap between NIT Trichy (#9 NIRF) and NIT Jamshedpur (#82). Recruiters absolutely know this. Goldman Sachs, Google, and DE Shaw recruit from 12–15 NITs, not all 31. The alumni network, research quality, and company access differ dramatically.

Reality Check
NITT ECE avg Rs.21.74L. NIT Jamshedpur CSE avg Rs.9–10L. Same NIT tag, very different outcomes.
Myth 04 · "AI Will Help CSE Graduates"

AI creates more CSE jobs for everyone

AI creates high-paying jobs for top CSE from top colleges but eliminates entry-level IT service roles where most lower-ranked NIT CSE graduates land. TCS plans to cut 12,000 mid-senior roles. AI coding tools reduce fresher developer headcount. The median TCS CSE fresher salary has barely moved in 5 years.

Warning
AI benefits the top 20% of CSE graduates. The bottom 60% who land in IT services face automation pressure.
Myth 05 · "Gulf Families Need IT Jobs"

Gulf NRI children must join IT companies

Gulf economies run on oil & gas, construction, energy, and manufacturing — all of which need Mechanical, Chemical, and ECE engineers. A Chemical Engg from NITT has a direct pipeline to ADNOC, Saudi Aramco, HPCL, BPCL that a lower-NIT CSE graduate cannot access, regardless of skills.

Reality Check
ADNOC/Aramco Chemical Engg packages: Rs.25–40L equivalent. Stronger than most IT service jobs.
Myth 06 · "Peer Pressure & Ego"

My child must say "I study CSE" at family gatherings

This is the real reason nobody admits. "CSE at NIT" sounds better in social settings than "Mechanical Engg at NIT Trichy." But the family that chose NITT Mech in 2020 now has a child at Goldman Sachs earning Rs.48 LPA, while the cousin at NIT Jamshedpur CSE is at TCS for Rs.7.5 LPA. Who is proud now?

The Real Threat · AI & Automation 2025–2030

What AI Is Actually Doing to IT Service Jobs

The picture is more nuanced than "AI creates jobs" or "AI kills jobs." Where you land in the food chain decides everything.

50,000 IT Service Jobs Vanishing in 2025

TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Tech Mahindra are conducting "silent layoffs" — no press releases, just quietly not renewing contracts and reducing fresher intake. AI automation replaces backend support, testing, and code maintenance roles that most service company freshers fill. TCS plans to cut 12,000 mid-senior roles specifically because AI handles what they used to do.

Where Jobs Are Growing: GCCs

Global Capability Centres (India now hosts 1,800+ GCCs — 55% of global total) are adding 4.25–4.5 lakh jobs in 2025 alone and will reach 3 million employees by 2030. GCC salaries are 25–30% higher than IT services. These are product-engineering, AI/ML, cybersecurity, and R&D roles — not body-shop outsourcing. They prefer NIT/IIT graduates with strong fundamentals over rote-trained service engineers.

The AI Skill Shift: What Companies Actually Want

Infosys hired 17,000 people in Q1 2025 and plans 20,000 more — but specifically for AI-driven growth, not traditional development. The jobs being created require AI/ML, cloud architecture, data engineering, and cybersecurity — skills built on strong Maths/algorithms foundations that ECE and CS both provide. A lower-ranked NIT CSE without these skills is no more competitive than a top-NIT ECE who builds them.

The "ECE Can't Do IT" Myth Destroyed

ECE students code more than people think

ECE curriculum at top NITs includes: C/C++ programming, Microprocessors (assembly), Embedded C, Python for Signal Processing, MATLAB, VHDL/Verilog for VLSI — easily 8–10 coding-related subjects. Add electives in ML/AI in Year 3–4 and the programming exposure is substantial.

The real difference: CSE graduates default to IT. ECE graduates can choose IT or core (VLSI, embedded, RF, telecom, EV, PSU via GATE). This optionality is more valuable, not less.

The Honest Verdict: Which Is Better for Your Future?

If you join a service company from a lower-ranked NIT CSE, your career trajectory is: TCS/Infosys → Rs.7 LPA → 3 years of repetitive work → try to switch → maybe Rs.15 LPA by Year 5 if you upskill hard. The AI automation wave is actively shrinking entry-level service roles. 50,000+ jobs vanished quietly in 2025.

If you take ECE/Mech/Chemical at a top-9 NIT (NITT) instead, you get: Day 1 placement at Qualcomm/L&T/Reliance → Rs.22–28 LPA → a real engineering career with deeper knowledge → multiple paths (product company, GATE/PSU, MS abroad, Gulf direct). The NITT brand unlocks GCC and product company access regardless of branch.

For Gulf NRI families specifically: Core engineering (Chemical, Mechanical, ECE) opens ADNOC, Saudi Aramco, HPCL, and Gulf infrastructure sectors that your family network can directly leverage. A lower-NIT CSE cannot access these.

Best for maximum salary: Product company

Requires top-15 NIT/IIT + strong DSA + competitive programming. Accessible from NITT CSE and ECE both — but not from lower-ranked NIT CSE.

Best for stable high salary + work-life: GCC

4.5 lakh jobs added in 2025. 25–30% higher than IT services. Accessible from top NITs across all branches with strong fundamentals.

Best for Gulf families: Core engineering at top NIT

NITT Chemical/Mech/ECE + Gulf family network = ADNOC/Aramco pipeline that CSE at any NIT cannot replicate.

Avoid: IT service companies without a plan

Joining TCS/Infosys without a clear upskill roadmap leads to the "AI replacement zone" by 2027. If this is the only option, have a 2-year upskill plan from Day 1.

Step-by-Step Guide · From JEE Rank to Dream Career

How Students Should Build Their Career Path

A branch-agnostic roadmap that works whether you take CSE at NITT, ECE at NITC, or Chemical at NITT — because the fundamentals of career-building are the same.

1

Choose College Brand Over Branch Name (Year 0)

NITT ECE > NIT Jamshedpur CSE. Every time. The college's NIRF rank, alumni network, and recruiter access determine outcomes more than branch. Choose the best institute your rank allows — don't sacrifice it for a branch name at a lower-ranked college.

2

Build Coding Skills Regardless of Branch (Sem 1–4)

All branches: master DSA (LeetCode/Codeforces), Python, Git, and at least one framework (Django/React/FastAPI). ECE students: learn Python alongside your circuit subjects. Mech students: learn CAD + Python for simulations. This is your "software passport" for any industry.

3

Choose Your Domain in Year 2 — Commit Fully (Sem 3–4)

By Semester 3, decide: software (DSA + competitive programming → product company), core (GATE prep → PSU/IIT M.Tech), hardware (VLSI/embedded → Qualcomm/TI path), or research (publications → MS abroad). Late decision = diluted preparation. Each path requires different Year 2 investments.

4

Secure a Meaningful Internship in Year 3 (Sem 5–6)

The Year 3 internship at NITT, NITC or any top NIT changes placement outcomes dramatically. Texas Instruments, Qualcomm, Goldman Sachs, Reliance all run intern programs. Pre-placement offers (PPOs) happen. Students who skip or ignore internship season miss their single biggest career accelerator.

5

CGPA + Projects = Your Interview Currency (All Years)

Product companies shortlist on CGPA ≥ 7.5 (most require 8.0+). GCCs use CGPA + project portfolio. GATE-aspirants need CGPA for IIT M.Tech shortlists. Build 2–3 strong GitHub projects by Year 3. One research publication by Year 4 dramatically improves MS abroad applications. Don't treat projects as extracurricular — they are core.

6

AI/ML Upskilling is Non-Negotiable by 2026 (Year 3–4)

Regardless of branch, every engineer in 2026 needs: Python fluency, understanding of ML basics (how models work), cloud fundamentals (AWS/GCP certifications are free for students), and the ability to use AI as a tool (GitHub Copilot, Claude, Gemini) to 10× productivity. This is not optional by 2027 — it is baseline.

7

Placement vs GATE vs MS: Decide by Sem 6 (Year 3)

Three career paths at graduation: (A) Campus placement → product/GCC company. Requires DSA prep + internship. (B) GATE → IIT M.Tech or PSU. Requires consistent GATE prep from Year 2. Mechanical/Chemical/EEE students have strong PSU pathways. (C) MS abroad → requires CGPA 8.5+, GRE, research paper, TOEFL. Don't try all three simultaneously — pick one by Sem 6 and execute fully.

8

Gulf Families: Leverage Your Network Explicitly

If your parent works in Gulf oil/gas/construction, this is a career asset most students waste. Chemical/Mech Engg from NITT + parent in Aramco/ADNOC/BAPCO = direct referral pathway that beats any campus placement. Tell your child's placement cell. Inform your parent's HR. Gulf industry referrals typically shortcut 2–3 years of career building. NITT Chemical engineers have used this path to reach ADNOC in 4–5 years post-graduation.
