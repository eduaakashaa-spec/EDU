The central NRI concern · Comfort-zone gap

Most NRI students are stepping into a different physical world

An honest conversation parents need to have before the child accepts a seat. We are not being negative about Indian campuses — we are being realistic about adjustment.

An AC home in the Gulf is a different physical reality from a non-AC hostel in central India.

The single most-repeated friction point in our research is climate-and-comfort adjustment. A child raised in air-conditioned Gulf homes, school buses and shopping malls — temperature 22-24°C year-round — arrives at an Indian campus where first-year rooms have a fan, three or four roommates, and summer dorm temperatures climbing past 40°C.

Add to that mess food calibrated to the dominant region (rice-and-sambar in NIT Trichy, dal-roti in MNIT Jaipur, coconut-oil-cooked Kerala food in NIT Calicut). For an outstation Gulf student, the first three months are real adjustment — sometimes the first year. This is normal. It is also worth preparing for.

NRI adjustment · The six friction points

Where the shift from Gulf to campus actually hurts

These six dimensions account for ~90% of NRI student complaints we observed across review platforms. None of them are dealbreakers — but each needs a conversation in the family before the seat is accepted.

A
Friction 01 · Physical comfort

AC / climate / cooler permission

Only NIT Delhi and DTU offer meaningful AC-room options at premium fees. Most institutes allow personal coolers; SVNIT Surat explicitly bans them in writing. Summers in Jaipur, Surat, Bhopal, Kurukshetra, Nagpur cross 45°C — non-AC hostel life is a hard adjustment for AC-raised children.

Tell parentsBuy a cooler in week 1; expect two weeks of disrupted sleep. By week 6, most adjust. By year 2, they're requesting non-AC rooms over AC.
F
Friction 02 · Food

Mess food + regional cuisine

South-Indian-heavy mess at NIT Trichy, NIT Calicut, IIIT Trichy. North-Indian-heavy at MNIT Jaipur, IIIT Allahabad. Coconut-oil-cooked at NIT-C. Veg-only at IIIT Pune, SMVDU. Outside-canteen access varies. Gulf students raised on diverse-cuisine homes often eat outside the mess for the first month.

Tell parentsPack a one-week emergency food kit. Plan a monthly off-campus restaurant budget. The food does improve once student finds their mess rhythm.
L
Friction 03 · Ladies' rules

Curfews + library hours + restrictions

NIT Kurukshetra (6 PM), NIT Calicut (7-9 PM circular controversies), MNNIT Allahabad (8:30 PM + library cut-off), NIT Trichy (9 PM strict), NSUT (8:30 PM + Wi-Fi cap). For NRI girls coming from gender-equal environments, these rules need an honest conversation.

Tell parentsVerify the latest curfew in writing from the admissions office. Some have softened in 2024-26; some have tightened. Don't trust 2020-era reviews on this point.
H
Friction 04 · Healthcare

Distance to a serious hospital

Best for healthcare access: DTU/NSUT (Delhi), UoH (Hyderabad — Apollo, Yashoda), IIIT Bangalore. Hardest: NIT Hamirpur, IIIT Una (small-town clinics + 200km drive), NIT Silchar (most isolated in the dataset), SMVDU Katra (pilgrimage town).

Tell parentsFor chronic conditions (asthma, diabetes, mental-health management) the institute's distance to a tertiary hospital is a real variable. Get the answer in writing.
P
Friction 05 · Airport

Gulf-direct flight proximity

Best: NIT Calicut (CCJ ~25 km), NIT Surathkal (IXE), IIIT Guwahati (~4 km!), DTU/NSUT/SPA Delhi (DEL), UoH (HYD), PEC Chandigarh (IXC). Worst: NIT Silchar, NIT Hamirpur, SMVDU Katra, IIITDM Jabalpur, IIIT Vadodara, ABV-IIITM Gwalior.

Tell parentsFamily-emergency travel time matters. A 4-hour drive after a 6-hour flight is a real friction. Pick proximity if other factors are equal.
C
Friction 06 · Culture

Language + regional politics

English is the working language of academics at every DASA institute — none is a barrier here. But socially: Hindi-belt at MNIT Jaipur, MANIT Bhopal; Telugu at NIT Warangal; Tamil at NIT Trichy, IIITDM Kancheepuram; Malayalam at NIT-C, IIIT Kottayam. JNU and UoH carry documented campus-political environments parents should know about.

Tell parentsOutside-classroom friendships often form along language lines for the first 6 months. By year 2, most outstation students have settled networks. Worth preparing for, not fearing.

Concrete safety incidents in the public record

Reported in mainstream press or by the institute itself. We list these because they exist, not to scare — every campus has issues; what matters is institutional response. Where the institute acted (expulsions, FIRs, policy changes), we note it.

Ragging assault on 3rd-year student

3rd-year student Rajan Das alleged hostel assault. Institute committee documented 45 students enduring "relentless ragging, physical and mental abuse". Main accused was a Bangladeshi student admitted via ICCR. Institute responded with 9 final-year expulsions; 5 additional suspensions in 2023.

Hostel suicide — father alleged ragging

3rd-year student Aditya Sohane found hanged in hostel; father told media he was "ragged after admission and had been disturbed since then". A second MCA-student suicide the same week. Pattern of student mental-health distress with parental ragging allegations.

Student stayed at bus stand "wary" of curfew

News story: rape of female student who stayed at a bus stand because she was wary of entering hostel past curfew and facing penalty. The case re-opened nationwide debate on hostel curfews as a safety issue rather than a safety solution.

2016 · NIT Calicut

"Don't roam with boys" circular

Hostel warden's written circular "strictly instructed" girls not to "roam with boys"; circular was withdrawn after national backlash. The episode is still cited as an example of paternalistic hostel rule-making.

Source · HuffPost · The News Minute · Feminism in India

Masked-mob attack on Sabarmati hostel

30+ students injured in masked-mob attack on Sabarmati and other hostels. Documented across major Indian and international press. Not a DASA-routine concern, but relevant for NRI parents evaluating JNU as it carries a politically charged campus environment that we surface honestly.

Ragging videos surfaced showing first-years stripped and forced to dance; Nepalese students publicly complained of "sadistic" ragging. Long since past, but still on public record. Institute's reputation has improved materially since then.

10 questions · For the admission visit

Ten things every NRI parent must ask the institute

Curfew, AC, mess, healthcare access, mentor institute, ragging history — get all in writing on letterhead. Verbal promises don't survive a year-on-year rule change.

Q1
Is AC available in any first-year hostel room? If yes — at what fee, with what waitlist, and which hostel? Show the latest hostel bulletin.
Q2
Are coolers / table fans permitted in hostel rooms? Confirm in writing — some institutes have written bans (notably SVNIT Surat).
Q3
What's the exact ladies' hostel curfew? First-year vs senior, weekdays vs weekends, weekend overnight-out policy. In writing.
Q4
Are library access hours equal for boys and girls? Asymmetric library hours exist at MNNIT Allahabad — a question worth asking elsewhere too.
Q5
Is the campus permanent or transit? IIIT Pune is still in transit; ask where my child will physically live in Year 1 through 4.
Q6
What's the nearest tertiary hospital? Distance in km, ambulance protocol, on-campus medical officer schedule.
Q7
Which is the nearest international airport with Gulf flights? Distance + drive time. Affects family-emergency travel.
Q8
What's the mess cuisine policy? Veg-only? Regional dominant? Outside-canteen access hours? Can my child opt out of mess (with refund)?
Q9
What's the institute's anti-ragging response history? Last 3 years of FIRs, expulsions, policy changes. Ask for the annual UGC anti-ragging report.
Q10
Can I have 3 current-student references? Phone or email. Make the calls before paying — ask each about food, safety, faculty.
