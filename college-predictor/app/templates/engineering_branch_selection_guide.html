Why this matters · For parents & students

A branch chosen well rarely fails. A branch chosen for the wrong reasons can quietly cost four years.

Many families pick a branch on trend, peer pressure, salary screenshots or last year's cut-off. A sound decision weighs the student, the college and the family — together.

Wrong-fit risk. A mismatched branch may not fail a student immediately — but it can create stress, low motivation, weak placements and career confusion after four years. The cost shows up late, when it is hardest to undo.

The real decision weighs three voices at once:

  • Student interest & aptitude
  • Maths / physics comfort
  • Coding interest
  • Communication skills
  • College placement ecosystem
  • Internship support
  • AI disruption to the role
  • Higher-studies plan
  • Family budget & ROI
  • NRI adjustment factors

Salary Reality · Skills decide the number

Salary is set by the profile, not the branch label

Two students in the same branch and college can land very different packages. The differentiator is the profile they build: projects, internships, communication and depth.

Tier 01

Degree only

Coursework done, few projects, weak portfolio. Placement is uncertain and pay tracks the floor.

Tier 02

Skilled graduate

Solid projects plus an internship. Reliable placements at moderate-to-good packages.

Tier 03

High-skill graduate

Strong domain depth, real coding or design work, certifications. Competes for higher offers.

Tier 04

Premium profile

Strong college + internships + projects + communication. The top band of offers opens up.

Read the median, not the headline. Salary figures move every year and the highest package is rarely typical. Before committing, ask each college for branch-wise placement percentage, the median salary, and verified internship data — that is the honest picture.
