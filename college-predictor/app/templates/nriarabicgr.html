Before any letter, the basics: how Arabic is read, why letters look different in different positions, and the three things every learner gets wrong on day one.

Three things to know before letter one

1

Arabic reads right-to-left

Books open from what English speakers call “the back”. Sentences flow from right to left. Numbers, oddly, still read left-to-right.

2

There are 28 letters

No vowels in the alphabet itself — vowels are added as small marks above or below letters. We’ll cover those in Module 7.

3

Most letters change shape based on position

A single letter has up to four forms: alone (isolated), at the start of a word, in the middle, and at the end. This sounds harder than it is — the shapes are predictable.

!
Common Mistake

Mistake: Trying to memorise all 4 forms of a letter before learning the next letter. Better: Learn the isolated form of all 28 letters first, then come back for forms in Module 6.
