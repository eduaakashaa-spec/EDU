Foundations · DASA & CIWG

Two paths into India's NITs that look identical — but aren't.

Most families discover the difference between DASA and CIWG only after they've started filling forms. Here's the plain-English version, before you commit to anything.

·01

DASA — Direct Admission of Students Abroad

The umbrella scheme run on behalf of the Ministry of Education by a rotating coordinating NIT (NIT Calicut for the 2026 cycle, expected). It opens seats at NITs, IIITs, SPAs and other CFTIs to NRIs, OCI/PIO and foreign nationals. Admissions are based solely on JEE Main 2026 All India Rank — SAT Subject Tests have been permanently discontinued and are no longer accepted.

  • Eligibility: valid JEE Main 2026 rank + Class XII (PCM, min. 75% aggregate or 7.5 CGPA).
  • Application is online at dasanit.org; registration fee USD 300 (non-refundable).
  • For NRIs: Class XI & XII must have been completed abroad (min. 2 years of study in last 8).
·02

CIWG — Children of Indian Workers in Gulf

A sub-category inside DASA carved out specifically for children of Indian-passport-holding workers in the six Gulf Cooperation Council countries (UAE, KSA, Oman, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait). The 2025 cycle filled 215 programs under this category. From 2021 onwards, CIWG admissions are ranked on the same JEE Main AIR — not SAT.

  • Tuition: ₹62,500 per semester in 2025 — roughly half of the Non-CIWG DASA seat fee.
  • Parent's GCC employment + Indian passport for both parent & child must be documented.
  • Same JEE Main rank pool, but separate merit list & closing ranks — usually more accessible than Non-CIWG.

Process · 6 steps · dasanit.org

The DASA 2026 admission journey, JEE Main first.

Since 2021 the Ministry of Education has made JEE Main the sole entrance exam for DASA and CIWG. SAT Subject Tests are permanently discontinued. Here is the exact 2026 sequence — expected dates from official communication so far; final calendar confirmed in the DASA 2026 brochure on dasanit.org.

Step · 01

Appear for JEE Main 2026

B.E./B.Tech → Paper 1. B.Arch → Paper 2A. B.Plan → Paper 2B. JEE Main is conducted twice (January & April sessions) by NTA; the better of the two scores is used. NRIs can write it from designated overseas centres including UAE.

Step · 02

Confirm eligibility

Born on or after 1 Oct 2001. Class XII passed (or appearing 2026) with PCM & min. 75% aggregate (7.5 CGPA), 70% / 7.0 for SC/ST. For NRIs: Class XI & XII abroad (min. 2 years of foreign schooling in the last 8). CIWG candidates additionally need GCC-employment proof for their parent.

Step · 03

Register on dasanit.org

Application window opens around late April / early May 2026 and stays open ~30 days. Use your JEE Main 2026 application number as username; activate via email link. Coordinating institute for 2026 is expected to be NIT Calicut (confirmed in the official brochure).

Step · 04

Documents & fee payment

Upload passport, JEE Main scorecard, Class X & XII marksheets, residence/study proof, and (for CIWG) parent's GCC visa + employer letter. Pay the non-refundable application fee of USD 300 via SWIFT / NEFT / card / UPI. Names must match exactly across passport, JEE Main and DASA form.

Step · 05

Choice filling & locking

Pick institute×branch combinations in your priority order from the DASA 2026 seat matrix — fill 40+ choices. Lock before the deadline; unlocked choices are not considered. A mock allotment runs before Round 1 so you can revise.

Step · 06

3 rounds → reporting

Rounds 1, 2 & 3 run from late June through July. After each allotment you choose Accept (locks the seat), Accept & Upgrade (keeps you in the running for a higher choice), or Decline (exits counselling). Pay first-semester tuition — ₹62,500 for CIWG, equivalent USD fee for Non-CIWG — and report to the allotted institute by late July / early August 2026.

FAQ · 9 questions · 2026 cycle

Questions Gulf-NRI families ask us every week.

Straight, fact-checked answers from the official DASA 2026 brochure, dasanit.org and NTA circulars. If yours isn't here, write to info@eduaakashaa.com.

Q · 01 · Most asked · 2026
Do I still need to write the SAT for DASA or CIWG in 2026?

No. The Ministry of Education made JEE Main mandatory for all DASA/CIWG admissions in 2021, and the College Board has since permanently discontinued SAT Subject Tests worldwide. JEE Main 2026 is the sole entrance exam for DASA UG 2026 admissions to NITs, IIITs, SPAs and other CFTIs — for NRIs, OCI/PIO, foreign nationals and CIWG candidates alike. The SAT plays no role.

Q · 02 · For students
Can my child write JEE Main 2026 from Dubai / Doha / Riyadh?

Yes. NTA conducts JEE Main twice a year (January & April sessions) and operates designated overseas test centres in the GCC including UAE (Dubai & Abu Dhabi), Bahrain (Manama), Kuwait, Oman (Muscat), Qatar (Doha) and Saudi Arabia (Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam), plus Kathmandu, Singapore, Colombo, Lagos and others. NRI candidates register on jeemain.nta.nic.in like everyone else and pick the nearest overseas centre. The better of the two session scores is used for the DASA merit list.

Q · 03 · For parents
Is my child eligible for CIWG, or only Non-CIWG DASA?

CIWG eligibility hinges on three things: (1) your child holds an Indian passport, (2) you (the parent) hold an Indian passport and are currently employed in one of the six GCC countries — UAE, KSA, Oman, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, (3) your child completed Class XI and XII abroad (min. 2 years of foreign schooling in the last 8). If any of these is missing, the only DASA route open is Non-CIWG (NRI Quota), which has significantly higher fees but typically slightly more competitive closing ranks.

Q · 04 · For students
How is my DASA / CIWG merit rank decided in 2026?

By your JEE Main 2026 All India Rank — nothing else. The Ministry of Education made JEE Main the sole entrance exam for DASA in 2021 and SAT Subject Tests have been permanently discontinued worldwide. DASA produces two separate ranked lists each year (CIWG and Non-CIWG / NRI Quota), but both are drawn from the same JEE Main score pool. The closing ranks shown across this page are JEE Main AIRs from the 2025 cycle.

Q · 05 · For parents
What's the real fee difference between CIWG and Non-CIWG?

For the 2025 cycle, the official fees were: CIWG seats at NITs ₹62,500 per semester tuition (approx. ₹1.25 lakh per year). Non-CIWG / DASA NRI seats were billed in USD — in the order of USD 4,000 per semester for SAARC candidates and roughly USD 8,000 per semester for non-SAARC at NITs (lower at SPAs & some IIITs). Add hostel and one-time admission charges. Both categories also pay the same USD 300 non-refundable application fee to DASA. 2026 figures are confirmed in the official brochure on dasanit.org — check before transferring funds.

Q · 06 · For students
My CIWG rank is 25,000 — what can I realistically expect?

At a JEE Main 2025 AIR of 25,000 in the CIWG list, you'd be competitive for a wide spread of NIT branches in core engineering (Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, EEE) at second-tier NITs, plus several IIITs in CSE-adjacent branches. CSE at top NITs like Trichy or Surathkal will be very tight. The predictor on this page will show your exact Safe, Moderate and Reach options — try it with your actual JEE Main rank.

Q · 07 · For parents
Is a DASA NIT seat treated equal to a JEE NIT seat after graduation?

Yes. The degree certificate is identical — same institute, same syllabus, same campus, same placement cell. Recruiters do not distinguish between DASA and JEE entrants on the degree itself. The only operational difference is the fee structure during the four (or five) years of study.

Q · 08 · For students
How many choices should I fill, and in what order?

Fill at least 40 choices. The rule we recommend: top-load with your Reach options (programs slightly above your rank — never fill in fear that you'll be auto-allotted something unwanted, since the system follows your order strictly), then Moderate, then Safe. Skipping the safe ones is a common mistake — a single round of bad luck and the seat slips. The predictor here splits your options exactly along these lines.

Q · 09 · For parents
Does the data on this page reflect 2026 admissions?

The closing ranks on this page are JEE Main 2025 All India Ranks from the actual 2025 DASA cycle — not projections. The 2026 ranks will be published by the coordinating institute (NIT Calicut, expected) on dasanit.org after each round. Historically, year-on-year closing ranks shift by 5–15% in either direction; treat the 2025 numbers as a strong baseline, not a guarantee. We refresh this page within 48 hours of each official 2026 round release.
