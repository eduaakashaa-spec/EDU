The Cost of Wrong Choice · Real Case Studies

When a branch choice goes quietly wrong

The most expensive mistake in Indian engineering admissions isn't choosing the "wrong college". It's choosing the wrong branch for the wrong reason. Six anonymised case studies from our last three counselling cycles — and the lessons families wish they'd learned in advance.

Case 01 · Tier-3 CSE for the brand
JOSAA 2022 · Chennai family

"Everyone said CSE — so we took it at any college that gave it."

Aarav had a JEE rank that could fetch CSE at a Tier-3 private college, or ECE at NIT Trichy. Family chose CSE — "no future in core branches," they were told. Two years in, internships were drying up. The college had no real software culture, no recruiter visits from product companies. He's now retraining via online bootcamps at age 23.

LessonBrand of college matters more than name of branch. Tier-1 ECE consistently outperforms Tier-3 CSE in placement data — including software roles.
Case 02 · Mechanical because "father is an engineer"
JOSAA 2021 · Mumbai family

"She just couldn't connect with thermodynamics and machine design."

Riya was strong at math and biology, loved teaching and writing. Family insisted on Mechanical at a top NIT — "engineering is the safer path." By Sem-3, her grades had collapsed; the visualisation-heavy curriculum didn't match her thinking style. She eventually dropped to a BBA + MBA route. Three lost years and significant cost.

LessonAptitude and interest matter more than family preference. Take an objective branch-fit assessment before JOSAA, not after Sem-3.
Case 03 · ECE forced when CSE was the fit
JOSAA 2020 · Bangalore family

"We told her 'take ECE as a hardware backup'. She wanted pure CSE."

Priya had been coding since 9th grade — built two apps, won a hackathon, knew her direction. Family worried about over-specialising and pushed ECE. She suffered through 4 semesters of analog circuits and electromagnetism that she had no interest in. Eventually moved to software via internships — but lost 18 months catching up on what plain CSE would have taught directly.

LessonIf the student has a genuine, demonstrated interest, trust the signal. "Backup branches" make sense for the undecided, not for those who already know.
Case 04 · Chemical for "stability" — without chemistry interest
JOSAA 2021 · Hyderabad family

"Chemical sounded stable. But he hated 11th-grade chemistry."

Karan had scored 92% in PCM with strong math but average chemistry. Family chose Chemical at a Tier-2 NIT because the closing rank fit. Within a year, lab sessions and reaction engineering became a battle. He eventually shifted to MBA via CAT — but only because he was top-quartile at math. A student without that fallback would have been stuck.

LessonBranch must match the subject you actually enjoyed in 11th–12th, not just the rank you got. Chemistry-light students rarely thrive in Chemical Engineering.
Case 05 · AI/DS at Tier-3 college on hype
JOSAA 2023 · Bengaluru family

"The brochure had 'AI' and a fancy lab photo. The reality was different."

Ananya chose "AI & Data Science" at a private college over a CSE seat at a strong NIT. The curriculum turned out to be plain CSE with two extra ML electives, no GPU access, no faculty research, no real ML internships. Her cousin at IIIT-H was already publishing papers and interning at Sarvam AI. Same year, vastly different trajectories.

LessonAI/DS is only as valuable as the college's research culture, GPU labs, faculty depth and recruiter access. Verify curriculum + placements, not just branch name.
Case 06 · Civil dismissed without checking modern reality
JOSAA 2022 · Coimbatore family

"We thought Civil meant 'no jobs'. We were wrong — by a decade."

Vikram had a strong interest in metros and smart cities but the family treated Civil as a "no scope" branch (a perception from 2014–18). They forced him into IT at a private college. He ended up unhappy in software and is now considering a Civil MS abroad — adding years and cost. Meanwhile NIT Civil graduates from his year are L&T metro project leaders.

LessonVerify perceptions with current placement data, not WhatsApp forwards. India's ₹100-lakh-crore infra pipeline is rewriting "no scope" stories for Civil.
