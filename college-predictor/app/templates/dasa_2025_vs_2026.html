Executive Summary · What Moved The big picture for 2026

Fees and the core CIWG framework are unchanged year-on-year, so the decisive shifts are structural: a leaner round structure, a wider and reshuffled institute pool, and an earlier, compressed timeline. Here is the at-a-glance read.

01 · 3 → 2

Fewer rounds

Brochure V1.3 confirms DASA 2026 “conducts a total of two rounds” — Round-III is dropped, and the CIWG vacancy reversion now happens at Round-II.

02 · 77 → 86

Bigger institute pool

Net +9 institutes: 5 new IIITs added and the GFTI/other list reshuffled (7 in, 3 out). NIT + IIEST count is unchanged at 31.

03 · US$ 4,300

Fees held flat

Registration US$ 300 + first-semester tuition US$ 4,000 (or US$ 2,000 with the SAARC 50% waiver) — identical to 2025. CIWG remains US$ 300 + ₹62,500.

04 · ~1 wk

Earlier & tighter

Registration opens 28 Jul 2026 (vs 30 Jul 2025) and the whole process wraps roughly a week earlier — with one round fewer to fit it in.

05 · 2000 → 2001

Age cut-off rolls on

Candidates must be born on or after 01 Oct 2001. Class XII must be 2025-26, with final results by 30 Sep 2026. Marks bar held at 75% / 7.5 CGPA.

06 · 5% / ⅓

CIWG intact

The 5%-within-15% (one-third of DASA seats) CIWG reservation and the 8 eligible Gulf countries are unchanged. Only the revert round and the parent-employment reference year move.

“01”
Change 01 · Process Structure Seat-allocation rounds & schedule

The single most consequential change for applicants. DASA 2025 ran three rounds; DASA 2026 (Brochure V1.3, 17 Jun 2026) runs only two — meaning fewer chances to upgrade a seat and a faster decision window.

What stayed identical

Registration fee remains non-refundable; full payment in one online transaction (card / net-banking / UPI / e-challan for INR); parents may pay in INR at the prevailing exchange rate; tuition-refund rules (Annexure 6) and the NRI CIWG-to-Non-CIWG refund mechanism are unchanged.

Counsellor Desk · Read Before Advising Flags & advisory notes

Points that need a counsellor’s judgement — including one internal inconsistency in the 2026 brochure that families should verify on the portal.

OK
Confirmed: DASA 2026 runs two rounds (V1.3)

Earlier 2026 drafts (V1.1) contained a contradiction — the schedule showed two rounds while clause 55 still read “three rounds.” Brochure V1.3 (17 Jun 2026) corrects this: it explicitly states DASA 2026 “conducts a total of two rounds,” consistent with the two-round Annexure 3 schedule and the Round-II CIWG reversion. Plan for exactly two rounds.

i
The institute list moved between brochure versions — use V1.3

The participating-institute list changed across 2026 versions. V1.1 had dropped Manipal Institute of Technology and IGDTUW; V1.3 (titled “Inclusion of New Institutions”) reinstated both, taking the total to 86. Always advise from the latest version and the live list on csab.nic.in.

!
Fewer rounds = front-load the choice list

With likely only two rounds, the cost of a poorly ordered choice list is higher — there are fewer opportunities to slide/float into a better seat. Lock a carefully ranked, realistic list before Round-I.

!
Re-plan if you were targeting a dropped institute

PDEU Gandhinagar, CHARUSAT and Puducherry Technological University are no longer in the DASA 2026 pool. Students must either secure them through the institute’s own NRI/management route or substitute equivalent newly-added options.

i
Verify the 75% on the JoSAA 2026 basis

2026 explicitly anchors the 75% / 7.5 CGPA to “aggregate in 5 subjects” per JoSAA 2026 Business Rule Annexure 2(b). Re-compute borderline candidates’ aggregates on the 5-subject basis before declaring eligibility.

Counsellor FAQ · DASA 2026

Questions families are asking

Does DASA 2026 really have only two rounds?
Yes — two rounds. Brochure V1.3 (17 Jun 2026) explicitly states DASA 2026 “conducts a total of two rounds,” and Annexure 3 lists only Round-I (result 06 Aug 2026) and Round-II (result 12 Aug 2026), with the CIWG vacancy reversion at Round-II. (An earlier draft, V1.1, had conflicting “three rounds” wording that has since been corrected.) DASA 2025, by contrast, ran three rounds.
Have the DASA fees increased for 2026?
No. The DASA Enrolment Fee is unchanged: US$ 300 non-refundable registration plus US$ 4,000 first-semester tuition (total US$ 4,300) for Non-SAARC; US$ 2,000 tuition (total US$ 2,300) for SAARC candidates with the 50% waiver; and US$ 300 + ₹62,500 for CIWG. NRI candidates opting to also be shown Non-CIWG seats still pay US$ 4,300.
Which institutes are new, and which were removed?
Newly added (12): IIIT Guwahati, IIIT Tiruchirappalli, IIIT Dharwad, IIIT Bhagalpur, IIIT Raichur, plus Guru Ghasidas Vishwavidyalaya (Bilaspur), NIFTEM Kundli, Central University of Jammu, Central University of Karnataka, Rajiv Gandhi National Aviation University, Islamic University of Science & Technology (Kashmir) and NITTTR Bhopal. Removed (3): PDEU Gandhinagar, CHARUSAT and Puducherry Technological University. Manipal Institute of Technology and IGDTUW were briefly dropped in draft V1.1 but reinstated in V1.3. Net total moves from 77 to 86 institutes (NIT+IIEST 31, IIITs 18, Other-GFTI 37).
Who is eligible by age and academics in 2026?
Candidates must be Foreign Nationals / OCI / PIO / NRI, born on or after 01 Oct 2001, with a valid JEE (Main) 2026 score, and at least 75% (or 7.5 CGPA) in aggregate over 5 subjects in Class XII with Maths, Physics and one of Chemistry / Biotechnology / Computer Science / Biology. Those appearing in 2025-26 may apply if final results are available by 30 Sep 2026.
Did anything change for Gulf-worker children (CIWG)?
The core benefit is unchanged: 5% of seats (within the 15% DASA supernumerary quota — about one-third of DASA seats) are reserved for CIWG across the same 8 Gulf countries (UAE, Bahrain, Iraq, Iran, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia). Two small updates: the parent-employment reference rolls to “any date of the year 2026,” and unfilled CIWG seats now convert to Non-CIWG at Round-II rather than Round-III.
